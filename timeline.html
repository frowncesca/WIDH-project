Ethel Reed

1874-1912

Ethel Reed studied at Cowles Art School, Boston in 1893. She gained recognition as a prominent poster artist, creating work for The Boston Herald. She moved to London in 1897, contributing to The Yellow Book, the controversial periodical co-founded by Aubrey Beardsley. In London she began affliations with the great creative figures of the period, including author Richard le Galliene, the architects Bertram Goodhue and Ralph Adams Cram and photographer Fred Holland Day.
